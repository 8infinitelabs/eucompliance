# LinkedIn Launch Post

## Version 1 — Technical/Developer audience

---

I just open-sourced something that doesn't exist anywhere else.

The EU AI Act (Article 16(l)) requires high-risk AI systems to be accessible under the European Accessibility Act. But no tool — open source or commercial — implements this.

AI governance tools ignore accessibility.
Accessibility tools ignore AI governance.

So we built the bridge.

eu-compliance-bridge is an open source toolkit (EUPL-1.2) that:

- Classifies AI systems by risk level (prohibited/high/limited/minimal)
- Maps AI Act obligations to EN 301 549 accessibility requirements
- Generates Fundamental Rights Impact Assessments with disability impact
- Produces Annex IV documentation and accessibility statements

Try it now:
npx eu-compliance-bridge classify "your AI system description"

Why does this matter?

- EAA enforcement started June 2025 — fines up to EUR 300K
- AI Act high-risk deadline is August 2026
- 160K+ entities affected across the EU
- Enterprise tools cost $15K-80K/year
- This is free and open source

Built with TypeScript, 37 tests, 5 packages.

GitHub: github.com/8infinitelabs/eu-compliance-bridge

If you're building AI systems in the EU, star the repo. If you work in disability rights or AI policy, we'd love your input on the regulatory mappings.

## Version 2 — Policy/NGO audience

---

Did you know the EU AI Act requires AI systems to be accessible to people with disabilities?

Article 16(l) creates a legal obligation that nobody is implementing.

A blind person evaluated by an AI welfare system should be able to:
- Understand the AI's decision (accessible explanation)
- Override or appeal it (accessible controls)
- File a complaint (accessible process)

But no tool connects AI governance with accessibility compliance.

We just open-sourced one: eu-compliance-bridge.

It's designed for NGOs, communities, and the public sector — not just corporations.

What makes it different:
- Generates AI Accessibility Impact Assessments (a methodology that didn't exist before)
- Maps AI Act articles to EN 301 549 clauses
- Includes disability impact in Fundamental Rights Impact Assessments
- Produces evidence usable for advocacy, not just internal compliance

Free. Open source. EUPL-1.2 license.

github.com/8infinitelabs/eu-compliance-bridge

Built by a solo developer in Barcelona who believes compliance tools shouldn't cost $50K/year.
